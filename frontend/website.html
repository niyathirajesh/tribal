The Only Tribal Kingdom of Kerala

The Mannan Community

The Mannan tribal community is a distinct ethnic group primarily inhabiting the Idukki district of Kerala, specifically within the Kovilmala forest ranges. They follow a sovereign monarchical system, headed by the Kovilmala Raja.

Social Organization

The tribe is divided into settlements managed by headmen (Kanis). The Raja acts as the supreme judge in resolving community disputes and preserving traditional laws.

"The Raja is elected through a democratic consensus among the elders, blending ancient concepts with modern participation."

Rituals & Ethnobotany

Mannan culture is tied to the Western Ghats. Their belief system is Animistic, honoring forest spirits and wild elephants.

Traditional Dance

The Koothu dance tells stories of ancestral heroes across several nights. Their songs serve as maps for medicinal plants and seasonal winds.

They possess an incredible knowledge of mountain survival, tracking, and the medicinal properties of forest flora.

A Kingdom in Exile

Legend says the Mannan were subjects of the Pandyan Kings of Madurai who migrated westward in the 14th century to escape invasions.

Colonial Resilience

The British recognized the Raja's authority in exchange for guidance through the dense forest terrains of Idukki. To this day, the Raja holds significant cultural authority and participates in state-level tribal council decisions.
